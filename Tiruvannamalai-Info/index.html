The Eternal Fire

History & Spiritual Significance

Tiruvannamalai is one of the most ancient and sacred sites in India. It represents the Agni (Fire) element among the five primordial elements (Pancha Bhoota Stalams) of Lord Shiva.

The word 'Arunachala' is derived from 'Aruna' (red/fire) and 'Achala' (hill). According to legend, Lord Shiva once appeared here as a column of fire that had no beginning or end, to quell the ego of Brahma and Vishnu.

The Arunachaleswarar temple is one of the largest in the world, with inscriptions dating back to the 9th-century Chola era.

Arunachaleswarar Temple

One of the largest temples in Tamil Nadu, dedicated to Lord Shiva as the element of Fire. The architecture is a marvel of Dravidian style.

View on Maps History & Significance

Divine Architecture

Dating back to the 9th century (Chola Dynasty), this temple complex spans over 25 acres. It represents Agni (Fire), one of the five elements.

Legend says Lord Shiva appeared here as an infinite column of fire (Lingodbhava) to quell the pride of Brahma and Vishnu. The temple has four massive gopurams (towers), with the Eastern tower (Raja Gopuram) standing at 217 feet, one of the tallest in India.

Sri Ramana Ashram

The sanctuary where the sage Ramana Maharshi lived. A place of deep silence and meditation at the foot of the hill.

View on Maps Spiritual Hub

Home of the Sage

Established in 1922 around the Samadhi of Ramana Maharshi's mother. The sage himself lived here until his Mahasamadhi in 1950.

It remains a global center for those practicing Self-Enquiry (Atma Vichara). The ashram retains the vibrating silence of the Master, offering a powerful environment for meditation. Peacocks roaming freely are a signature sight.

Virupaksha Cave

A small cave on the eastern slope of Arunachala where Ramana Maharshi meditated for 17 years. It has a powerful spiritual aura.

The 'Om' Shape

This cave is naturally shaped like the sacred symbol 'Om'. It is named after the 13th-century saint Virupaksha Deva.

Sri Ramana Maharshi stayed here from 1899 to 1916. It is said that the intense spiritual heat of his inquiry was so strong here. It remains one of the most powerful meditation spots on the Holy Hill.

Giri Pradakshina

Walking around the hill clockwise is considered equivalent to circling Lord Shiva himself. The path is dotted with 8 Cardinal Lingams (Ashta Lingams) and numerous shrines.

Significance: It dissolves karma and fulfills righteous desires. Traditionally done barefoot. The full moon (Pournami) sees lacs of devotees, but the walk is sacred on any day.

Scenic Hermitage

Ramana Maharshi moved here from Virupaksha Cave in 1916 and stayed until 1922. His mother joined him here, and her liberation (Mukti) occurred in this very spot.

It offers a breathtaking view of the Arunachaleswarar Temple towers. The atmosphere is exceptionally cool with a perennial mountain spring nearby.

Sathanur Dam

A major dam built across the Thenpennai River, featuring a beautiful park and a crocodile farm. A great picnic spot.

Engineering Marvel

Constructed in 1958, this is one of Tamil Nadu's major dams. The surrounding park is often used as a film shooting location for its scenic gardens and statues.

It houses a large Crocodile Bank and a fish grotto. Best visited during the post-monsoon months when water levels are high.

Complete Glossary

Tiruvannamalai A-Z

A comprehensive guide to the spiritual terms and places of the holy town.

A

Annamalaiyar

The presiding deity Lord Shiva, worshipped as the element of fire.

B

Bharani Deepam

Lit inside the temple at 4 AM on Karthigai Deepam day, symbolizing the One Source.

C

Chitrai Pournami

A full moon in the month of Chithirai (April/May) highly auspicious for Girivalam.

D

Deepam

The Great Light. The Karthigai Deepam festival attracts millions to see the flame atop the hill.

G

Girivalam

Giri (Hill) + Valam (Rounding). The act of circumambulating the holy hill (14km).

I

Idai Kattu Siddhar

One of the 18 Siddhars believed to still dwell in spiritual form around the hill.

K

Karthigai

The Tamil month (Nov-Dec) when the Great Festival takes place.

L

Lingam

The aniconic representation of Lord Shiva. There are 8 Cardinal Lingams on the path.

M

Maha Deepam

The huge fire lit at sunset on top of the 2,668ft high mountain during the festival.

P

Pournami

Full Moon Day. The most powerful time for Girivalam, drawing massive crowds.

R

Ramana Maharshi

The great sage of Arunachala who revived the path of Self-Inquiry in the 20th century.

S

Siddhars

Realized saints and mystics, many of whom are believed to reside in the caves of Arunachala.

T

Thiruvoodal

A festival in Jan/Feb celebrating a divine dispute between Shiva and Parvati.

V

Virupaksha

An ancient cave and the name of a saint who lived there in the 1300s.

Plan Your Spiritual Journey

Everything you need to know for a peaceful and fulfilling visit to Tiruvannamalai.

By Air

Chennai International Airport (170km) is the nearest major airport. Bangalore Airport is also a viable option (200km).

Accommodation

From peaceful ashram guest houses like Sri Ramanasramam to luxury boutique resorts, find the perfect stay for your spiritual retreat.

Local Transport

Auto-rickshaws are plentiful for short trips. For the 14km Girivalam, many pilgrims walk, while others rent bicycles or electric scooters.

Food & Dining

Savor authentic South Indian vegetarian meals. Many organic cafes around Ramanasramam serve international cuisines for global seekers.

Health & Safety

Carry basic medicines and sun protection. Several multi-specialty hospitals are available in the town center for emergencies.

Ideal Duration

A minimum of 2-3 days is recommended to cover the Temple, Ashram, and the sacred Girivalam circumambulation.

Best Time to Visit

Winter (Nov - Feb) offers pleasant weather (15C-30C). Karthigai Deepam (Nov/Dec) is the grandest festival.

Expert Travel Tips

  • Start Early: Begin Girivalam before dawn (4 AM) to avoid the heat.
  • Respect Silence: Maintain silence in meditation halls and caves.
  • Connectivity: 4G/5G works well, but Ashrams often have limited Wi-Fi.
  • Girivalam Apps: Use local apps to track your distance and landmark stops.
  • Monkeys: Keep food items inside bags to avoid attracting curious monkeys.
  • Shoes: You must leave shoes outside temples; carry a light cloth bag for them.
  • Cash: Keep some cash handy as small shops might not accept digital payments.
  • Dress Code: Shoulders and knees should be covered when entering temples.
